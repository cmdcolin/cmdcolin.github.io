<h2 id="the-rule"><a aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#the-rule"><a href="#the-rule" style="margin-right: 10px">#</a></a>The rule</h2>
<p>There is a neat typescript-eslint rule called
<code>@typescript-eslint/no-unnecessary-condition</code> that will tell you, according to
the types available at the time, to remove 'unnecessary if statements' (or
similarly, unnecessary usages of nullish coalescing, etc.)</p>
<p>I realized the existance of this rule when I upgraded from the
<code>recommended-type-checked</code> ruleset to the <code>strict-type-checked</code> rule set.</p>
<p>This is a good hint to crank up typescript-eslint strictness as much as you can.
Even if you have to disable some of the rules, it is full of goodies to turn on
by default!</p>
<h2 id="whats-the-catch"><a aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#whats-the-catch"><a href="#whats-the-catch" style="margin-right: 10px">#</a></a>What's the catch?</h2>
<p>I think in order to use this rule correctly, you should make
<code>noUncheckedIndexedAccess: true</code> in your tsconfig.json. I have previously
written about this tsconfig setting here
<a href="https://cmdcolin.github.io/posts/2023-09-27-areyousure">https://cmdcolin.github.io/posts/2023-09-27-areyousure</a> and my previous
conclusion was to make silly hacks in order to not enable it, but I think it is
better if you DO enable it</p>
<p>This is because the "no-unnecessary-condition" rule will otherwise hit on valid
undefined checks.</p>

<h2 id="what-should-you-expect-after-enabling-everything"><a aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#what-should-you-expect-after-enabling-everything"><a href="#what-should-you-expect-after-enabling-everything" style="margin-right: 10px">#</a></a>What should you expect after enabling everything?</h2>
<p>After you enable <code>noUncheckedIndexedAccess</code> above, you may have to add some well
placed non-null assertions (e.g. the !) which is no worse than what your code
was before around indexed accesses, or alternatively change your code to handle
the new undefined part of the types.</p>
<p>In a small number of cases, I had to keep a couple of superstitions if's alive
with a couple
<code>// eslint-disable-next-line for @typescript-eslint/no-unnecessary-condition</code>
but these are generally due to my types not being well done, and is a signal to
improve the types!</p>
