#The rule

There is a neat typescript-eslint rule called @typescript-eslint/no-unnecessary-condition that will tell you, according to the types available at the time, to remove 'unnecessary if statements' (or similarly, unnecessary usages of nullish coalescing, etc.)

I realized the existance of this rule when I upgraded from the recommended-type-checked ruleset to the strict-type-checked rule set.

This is a good hint to crank up typescript-eslint strictness as much as you can. Even if you have to disable some of the rules, it is full of goodies to turn on by default!

#What's the catch?

I think in order to use this rule correctly, you should make noUncheckedIndexedAccess: true in your tsconfig.json. I have previously written about this tsconfig setting here https://cmdcolin.github.io/posts/2023-09-27-areyousure and my previous conclusion was to make silly hacks in order to not enable it, but I think it is better if you DO enable it

This is because the "no-unnecessary-condition" rule will otherwise hit on valid undefined checks.

#What should you expect after enabling everything?

After you enable noUncheckedIndexedAccess above, you may have to add some well placed non-null assertions (e.g. the !) which is no worse than what your code was before around indexed accesses, or alternatively change your code to handle the new undefined part of the types.

In a small number of cases, I had to keep a couple of superstitions if's alive with a couple // eslint-disable-next-line for @typescript-eslint/no-unnecessary-condition but these are generally due to my types not being well done, and is a signal to improve the types!
