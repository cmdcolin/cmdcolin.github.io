In my previous post I mentioned http://www.encodestatistics.org This site introduces IDR but also has something called GSC - ( Block Bootstrap and Segmentation )

Bootstrapping techniques are a new thing to me, so I didn't really grasp the GSC concept at first, but I found another blog post about bootstrapping for a different subject on the bioinformatics subreddit.

The post in question is called "Sampling a matching distribution for bootstrapping" and it contains some new ideas about bootstrapping that I found very interesting

This is a "case in point" figure from the post.

Fig. "Here are histograms I created based on CEU allele frequencies from HapMap Phase 3 for GWAS catalog SNPs (as of Jan 16, 2013) vs. all SNPs included in the HapMap SNP chips....pretty different distributions huh?" from Sampling a matching distribution for bootstrapping

This difference in the distribution of the GWAS SNP frequencies in a population  makes it difficult to know if the crucial GWAS genes are being overrepresented in the regions that you are sampling. therefore, the frequencies are sampled relative to the distribution of all SNPs.

Another interesting method was used for choosing a nearby SNP as a control SNP for each GWAS SNP. Then, the control SNP would act as a control by having a different random frequency compared with the GWAS SNP, so if you find that the QTLs are enriched relative to the controls, then you remove the chance that the GWAS frequency was influencing the results

Now that I understand the concept I think that looking at bootstrapping more closely with the ENCODE package GSC will be more likely (at least statistically more likely :))))
